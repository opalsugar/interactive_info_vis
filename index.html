Context

This prototype allows users to plan their morning at the start of the day or the night before, starting at 6am and going till 10am. The point of this prototype is to allow users to understand how long their morning routine tasks actually take them, and dynamically show them when they'll be done with their tasks based on how long it actually takes them to complete each task.

Design Decisions
  • Tabular Format: This clock follows a tabular format where each task fills up slowly over time. This is similar to a google calendar setup that time blockers commonly use, making it intuitive to what users may already be used to and allows the actual and intended plan to be easy to read. One constraint is that the timer does not automatically check actual time and is limited to 6 to 10am.
  • Pastel Colors: For each task, colors are randomly generated using RGB values but will always be light or pastel colored. This guarantees that the text inside each task will always be readable regardless of the randomly generated task color.
  • Interaction: Rather than using a task dictionary, this prototype allows users to add their own task names and task times to the calendar, making it fully customizable to their morning routine.
Future Work
  • Setting Time Frame: Making it so the user can decide on the total time interval. For example, they could select 5 hours and the clock auto adjusts to be from 6am to 11am.
  • Tracking Actual Time: A good future change could be making it so the user inputs a total time frame and then when they click "Start", it auto-adjusts to the starting time and time frame. For example, a user can select 5 hours and start the timer at 8am, and the clock adjusts to be from 8am to 1pm.

Sketch Narrative

Context

This prototype is focused on creating some space in a person's morning routine to focus on mindfulness by combining it with their morning coffee. It includes a calming 5-minute meditation focused on morning coffee sourced from YouTube, where the user focuses on their breathing and sipping their drink to get some relaxation in even during busy mornings.

Design Decisions
  • Coffee Cup Design: To add to the aesthetic of morning meditation, I decided to use the visual of a coffee cup emptying as the timer. This is to subtly encourage the user to take sips of their drink as they participate in the meditation, allowing them to feel present with both the meditation and with their cup.
  • Meditation Audio: Initially I was just planning to make this a timer, but based on feedback from my roommate I decided to find a coffee meditation online and embed it into the timer. This creates a relaxing atmosphere with a guided audio telling the user how to breathe, what to focus on and when to sip their drink, enhancing the immersion and relaxation gained from the experience.
  • Start, Pause and Reset Buttons: Rather than just making it a simple timer that counts down, I wanted the user to not feel obstructed when using the timer, giving them the option to pause if something comes up, restart if they feel interrupted, or end the meditation early.
Future Work
  • Different Coffee Drinks: Adding different types of drinks would allow the user to customize their experience based on their favorite drink in the morning, enhancing the visual appeal and aesthetic experience. This was suggested from class feedback.
  • Different Meditations and Times: An addition to the first idea could also be having different meditation audios and timers for different drinks, some being longer and shorter and maybe themed around the drink, both enhancing customizability and appeal.

Sketch Narrative

Context

This prototype is focused on encouraging users to take shorter showers, both to increase the amount of time they have in the mornings and also to be sustainable with their water usage. When the timer ends, an alarm goes off, but the timer continues and the tub overflows to show excess water usage and to track how long the user's shower lasts.

Design Decisions
  • Time Selection:For this timer, I wanted a constraint of 5 minutes without customizability as the whole point was to encourage shorter showers to save time, water and energy.
  • Sustainable Colors:I decided to make all text blue to complement the aesthetic of sustainability and saving water, with the timer turning red if it goes into the negatives to indicate overuse of water.
  • Visual and Auditory Design: Initially, the only planned feedback for going over time would be to have the water in the tub start to overflow. However, I got some feedback from the class that it would be difficult for a person to know they went over if they were in the shower, so I also added auditory feedback where an alarm goes off when 5 minutes are up and the timer is going into the negatives.
Future Work
  • Daily Shower Tracking: An interesting addition could be a calendar where users have a way to track how long their showers are everyday and how much water they're using or saving. It could be a good way to incentivize consistently taking shorter showers.
  • Multiple Alarms: Currently, there is only one alarm when the timer goes past 5 minutes. It could be interesting to have multiple timers, like one after 3 more minutes, then after 2, and then after 1 and so on. This could help create a sense of urgency for the user and make them speed up to reduce the length of their shower.
